Pasta Carbonara

Pasta carbonara is a typically Italian recipe made with pecorino and bacon. This one will transport you to Italy as if you were really there!

Ingredients

  • 200g Spaghetti
  • 100g Pancetta
  • 50g Pecorino
  • 2 Eggs
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Preparation

  1. Start by filling a large pot with water, don't skimp on the quantity, as they say in Italy "pasta needs space".
  2. Heat the water with a small handful of coarse salt.
  3. While waiting for the water to boil, cut the guanciale into small cubes (if you don't have any, substitute with pancetta) and fry them in a frying pan (not oiled!). Stop cooking when the guanciale is firm and golden.
  4. The water should now boil. While the pasta cooks, beat the eggs with a fork by adding the pepper, the salt (very little) and a handful of pecorino.
  5. When the pasta is cooked (between 10 and 12 minutes, try regularly) drain normally. Traditionally and ideally they should be "althed".
  6. Be careful, do not pass the saucepan under water and put it back on the stoves (off). She must stay warm.
  7. Transfer the pasta into the frying pan with the guancial and gently mix with a wooden spoon so that "pasca" is impregnated with the fragrance.
  8. Pour the whole thing into the saucepan (so still hot) and add the beaten eggs.
  9. Mix so that the eggs take lightly. Let us re-declare that the latter must not really cook and this operation therefore runs well off.
  10. Finally, second the taste, optionally add pepper and pecorino.
  11. Serve hot.
